on-chain checks

Straight from the Solana chain, on every scan:

  • mint authority — active means the dev can print unlimited new supply. Instant red flag.
  • freeze authority — active means your tokens can be frozen the moment you buy (honeypot risk). Instant red flag.
  • top-10 holder concentration — % of supply held by the 10 largest accounts (liquidity pools and bonding curve accounts included, and noted as such). ≥50% is a red flag, ≥30% a warning.

market sanity

Market structure with explicit thresholds — no vibes, published numbers:

  • liquidity is summed across every pool where the token trades, so big tokens aren't under-counted
  • liquidity under $1,000 → red flag (you may not be able to sell)
  • liquidity below 2% of market cap → red flag (easy rug / heavy slippage); below 5% → warning
  • 24h volume below 1% of market cap (or under $100) → possible dead token
  • market cap under $100k → micro-cap warning; fewer than 200 holders → tiny community warning
  • pair younger than 7 days → no track record warning (and a hard cap on the score)
  • pump.fun tokens: bonding curve % or Raydium migration status

Hard caps keep the number honest: two or more red flags cap the score at 35, one caps it at 65. And no track record means no LOW rating: a pair younger than 2 days can never score above 55, younger than 7 days never above 75 — renounced authorities and a clean distribution are table stakes on pump.fun, not proof of anything. Risk level: ≥80 LOW · 50-79 MEDIUM · <50 HIGH. The score is always the last section of the report.

meta radar

HOOPIUM pulls the tokens currently live and trending on pump.fun — a real-time snapshot of the market meta — plus the scanned coin's own lore/description, and the report judges whether the narrative fits what's running right now. A clean token in a dead meta is still a bad trade; timing the meta is half the game.

x account history

Drop an @username (or a bare handle) in the terminal instead of a CA and HOOPIUM checks the account's history against the Wayback Machine's public archives — no X API, no paid data:

  • confirmed minimum age — the oldest archived snapshot of the profile. The account can only be older than that, never younger.
  • tweet count, then vs now — extracted from archived profile pages where the markup allows it, compared against the current live profile (or the newest snapshot when X blocks the live page)
  • possible mass tweet deletion — flagged only when the current count dropped more than 30% AND by at least 100 tweets vs an old snapshot; small deletions and parsing noise never trigger it
  • archived identity — display name and bio from the oldest and newest snapshots, so you can see if the account was rebranded

Everything the check could not verify is listed in a NOT CONFIRMED section instead of being guessed — no snapshots means "nothing confirmed either way", not "fresh account". This is best-effort by nature: X blocks direct scraping and archived HTML varies wildly across the years, so the data may be incomplete, and every report says so.

your buy rules

Define your own thresholds over any scanned metric and get an instant WOULD BUY / WOULD NOT BUY verdict on every scan, before the report renders. Two ways to build them:

  • simple: pick a metric, an operator (>= <= > < ==) and a value
  • setups: describe your criteria in plain english and the AI compiles them into a named set of conditions the coin must comply with to be tradable or get a score
> "at least $50k liquidity, 500+ holders, top 10 under 25%, mint renounced"
⚙ degen scalp — 4 conditions
  ✓ liquidity >= $50,000
  ✓ total holders >= 500
  ✓ top 10 holders <= 25%
  ✓ mint authority renounced

Conditions can reference any scanned metric: hoopium score, market cap, liquidity, liquidity/mcap ratio, 24h volume, price and 24h change, pair age, top-10 holder concentration, total holders, rugcheck score, bonding curve progress, and mint & freeze authority status.

Name a setup once, describe it once — then edit, toggle or delete it anytime from the panel. A condition referencing missing data returns UNKNOWN and never counts as passing: missing information is treated as risk, not as a pass. Rules and setups are saved in your browser.

chrome extension

A mini HOOPIUM terminal in your toolbar: copy a CA on Axiom, Photon, BullX, DexScreener or pump.fun and scan without switching tabs — or select the address on any page, right click, "Scan with HOOPIUM", and the popup opens with the report already loading. download the extension (.zip) and see the install steps.

report anatomy

  • HARD DATA — the numbers
  • RED FLAGS — worst first
  • COMPARABLES — how the lookalikes are doing
  • META FIT — the current meta and whether this coin belongs in it
  • YOUR RULES — your criteria, your verdict
  • GUT TAKE — would HOOPIUM ape or not (automated opinion, never advice)
  • HOOPIUM SCORE — X/100 — RISK: LEVEL

If the write-up model is unavailable, the raw computed scan is returned instead — the analysis never depends on the writer. External hiccups and rate limits are reported in the chat, never as a crash.
