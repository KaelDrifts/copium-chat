the mission

HOOPIUM has one single mission: scanning Solana tokens for rug vibes before you ape. Trained on thousands of hours of market data from the trenches, it compresses the whole due-diligence loop — authorities, holders, liquidity, volume, bonding curve, narrative — into one action: paste a contract address (CA), get a risk report in seconds.

Every day thousands of tokens launch. Deployers know everything; buyers get a ticker, a chart and whatever the deployer typed into a description box. The data to level that asymmetry is public, but it's scattered across four different tools — and in the trenches, four tabs is the whole trade. HOOPIUM runs the full checklist for you, automatically, on every scan.

how a scan works

  1. you paste a CA (base58, 32-44 chars) — an @username routes to the account history check instead, anything else gets bounced back
  2. HOOPIUM pulls market structure from DexScreener: price, market cap, 24h volume, pair age, and liquidity summed across every pool where the token trades
  3. it checks the chain itself: mint & freeze authority status and top-10 holder concentration
  4. for pump.fun tokens: bonding curve progress, or whether it already graduated to Raydium — plus the token's own lore/description
  5. it cross-references RugCheck's independent risk score and pulls 3-5 similar-named tokens, because most launches are clones and the clones tell you how the story ends
  6. it reads the current market meta from what's live and trending on pump.fun right now, and judges whether this coin's narrative fits it
  7. an ML model — trained through countless hours of pattern recognition in the trenches — turns all of that into red flags and a 0-100 HOOPIUM score, your own buy rules get evaluated, and the report is written up in the terminal

x account receipts

Half the rugs in the trenches start with a "dev" account created last week. Drop an @username in the terminal and HOOPIUM digs through the Wayback Machine's public archives instead of the chain: the oldest archived snapshot of the profile is the account's confirmed minimum age, the archived display name and bio show whether it was rebranded, and the tweet count then vs now gets compared — with a possible mass deletion flag only when the drop is big enough to be real evidence. X blocks direct scraping, so it's best-effort by design: whatever couldn't be verified is listed as NOT CONFIRMED, never guessed. No X API, no paid data.

two verdicts, not one

Every scan ends with HOOPIUM's verdict — the 0-100 score with a LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH risk level, computed by ML and countless hours of pattern recognition — and your verdict: an instant WOULD BUY / WOULD NOT BUY against the setups you defined yourself — named sets of conditions the AI compiles from a plain-english description of your criteria. HOOPIUM's opinion never overrides yours; you get both, side by side.

what it does NOT do

No financial advice. No price predictions. No trading signals. The gut take at the end of each report is an automated opinion produced by the model, clearly labeled as such. Renounced authorities and clean distributions do not make a token safe, and public data can be wrong, stale or gamed. Nothing in HOOPIUM is a reason to buy a token. Always dyor, ser.
